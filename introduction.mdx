Ancra is a Dedicated Virtual Account (DVA) infrastructure API built for the Nigerian payments ecosystem. With Ancra, you can provision real bank account numbers for your customers, automatically credit their ledger balances the moment a payment arrives, and send money to any Nigerian bank account — all through a single, consistent REST API.

Whether you are building a fintech wallet, a marketplace escrow system, or a B2B payments product, Ancra gives you the low-level primitives you need to move money safely and reliably.

## How It Works

<Steps>
  <Step title="Create a Customer">
    Register a customer record in Ancra. Every virtual account is scoped to a customer, so this is always your first step.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Provision a Virtual Account">
    Request a Dedicated Virtual Account for the customer. Ancra returns a real Nigerian bank account number that the customer can share with anyone who needs to pay them.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Receive a Payment via Webhook">
    When a NIP transfer arrives at the virtual account, your configured webhook URL receives a `payment_success` event. Ancra verifies the provider signature before delivering the event to you.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Ledger is Credited">
    Ancra applies a double-entry credit to the customer's ledger balance. You can query the balance at any time or act on it programmatically inside your webhook handler.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Initiate a Transfer">
    When the customer is ready to send money, call the transfers endpoint. Ancra debits the ledger and forwards the instruction to the payment provider. If the transfer is rejected, the debit is automatically reversed.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Base URL

All API requests are made to your own deployment. Replace the example below with your actual deployment URL.

```
https://your-deployment.onrender.com
```

<Note>
  All monetary amounts in the Ancra API — balances, transfer amounts, and payment values — are expressed in **kobo**, the smallest unit of the Nigerian Naira. 1 NGN = 100 kobo, so ₦1,000 is represented as `100000`.
</Note>
